Creating a collage is like crafting a visual symphony, where disparate elements harmonize to tell a story, evoke emotions, or capture a moment frozen in time. Whether meticulously arranged or haphazardly layered, collages offer a unique canvas for artistic expression, inviting viewers to explore the interplay between images, textures, and meanings. At its core, a collage is a collage is a compilation of diverse materials—photographs, magazine clippings, fabric scraps, found objects—woven together to form a cohesive whole. Its origins trace back to the early 20th century, when avant-garde artists such as Pablo Picasso and Georges Braque pioneered the use of collage as a radical departure from traditional artistic conventions. .
